About The Sultan's Palace

The Sultan's Palace, located in New Orleans, Louisiana, is a scary, haunted attraction that is a perfect place to visit in the month of October. Sultan's Palace is located on 716 Dauphine Street in the famous French Quarter, and is only minutes from nightlife and restaurants. Sultan's Palace has the legend surrounding it that a mass murder was committed here many years ago, causing the palace to have a great amount of paranormal activity surrounding it. The beauty of the palace, the creepy story of murder, and the location in the gorgeous city of New Orleans makes this haunted attraction a must see for people of all ages.

The History

In 1836, Sultan's Palace was built on the corner of Dauphine Street and Orleans Avenue. This luxurious Greek home was bought by a Turkish man and at night, the house came alive with music, young women, and parties. As suspicion of the palace grew, one day there was blood seen seeping down the walls, steps, and sidewalks. What they found in the house was even worse. Women, men, and children's body parts were found. Even Sultan's brother was found buried alive in the backyard, and was suspected to be killed by Sultan. The palace house is now said to be haunted because of this mass murder.
