Biography

Margaret E. Knight was an American inventor of machines and mechanisms born in York, Maine, in the mid 1900's. From an early age, Margaret had demonstrated an interest in tools and invention, with her first invention being created when she was only 12! Since then, Margaret has invented 90 things and had 27 patents. Many of her famous inventions we use almost everyday.

Margaret Knight's inventions include a numbering machine, a window frame and sash, several devices relating to rotary engines, and also a brown paper bag. Margaret invited the famous brown paper bag. She also created a machine that could create the square-bottomed grocery bag that is still used today. Though she failed to receive much profit off of her inventions, her inventions had a large impact on the world. Up onto her death in October of 1914, Margaret still invented many objects. After her death, Margaret was honored in a local obituary as a "woman Edison."
